Why Playing The Ball Off Your Left Foot Isn't Enough To Hit Longer Drives

By: Dustin D. May 3, 2026 In Partnership With Gentle Gator Golf

Don't underestimate your set up. Small changes add up to big results.

I used to think that as long as I played the ball off my left heel, I was doing everything I needed to maximize my distance. We've all heard it: "Put the ball forward in your stance to hit it further." Its something most golfers do without question.

But no matter how far forward I moved the ball up, my drives were still ballooning and falling short with almost zero roll out. No matter how much I tried to "feel" like I was swinging up, I just couldn't get the ball to launch higher with the knuckle spin we all chase. I had to change something else...

Hitting Up = Less Spin = More Distance

Hitting down on the ball is a distance killer resulting in a lower launch angle with high spin costing golfers serious yardage.

How I Gained 30 Yards By Tweaking My Set Up

1. Right Foot Drop Fix

Most golfers stand with their feet too close together. When your stance is narrow, you end up "stacked" on top of the ball like a short iron. This forces a steep descent, resulting in weak, spinning slices. You can see the tip of Drive Align is passed the ball as an indicator

To fix your launch angle, drop your right foot back until the inside of your foot is shoulder-width apart. This creates the platform needed to swing up on the ball. Don't overdo it, or you'll bottom out early and "top" the ball. Drive Align will point through the center of the ball when set up correctly

Drive Align is great for seeing "how much" is enough when adjusting your set up

2. Spine Tilt

Adjust your spine to tilt slightly away from the ball. You want you left shoulder higher than your right. This encourages an upward swing path and helps you hit up on the ball. Don't go overboard with tilt with the correct stance width this should only be a little bit.

3. The Visual Illusion No One Tells You

The hardest part is to overcome the visual illusion that tricks golfers into hitting slices when set up correctly. Your head will now be much further back than you are used to. Making aiming the driver really difficult. To add to the problem driver faces are curved with no flat edges. Squaring one up by eye is literally a guessing game. You might feel aligned, but your eyes are lying to you.

Drivers have no flat edges making it impossible to aim without a lot of trial and error. Drive Align makes training your eyes to see a square face fast and easy

4. The "Six Degree" Disaster

One tiny "tick" on a clock face is 6 degrees. At driver speeds, that tiny error sends your ball over 40 yards off target. If you can't see exactly where you are aimed, you've lost the hole before you swing.
