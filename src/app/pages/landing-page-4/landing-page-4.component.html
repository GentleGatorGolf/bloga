The Mistake That's Right In Front of Your Eyes And It's Killing Your Drives

By Harley H May 3, 2026 In Partnership With Gentle Gator Golf

In golf, a tiny mistake at address leads to a huge miss down the fairway. Being off by just 1 degree at impact is the difference between a perfect drive and a ball lost in the woods. For most golfers, the problem isn't their swing—it's that they are aiming their clubface at the wrong target without even knowing it.

1 minute on a clock = 6° = 48 yards offline

The Problem

The Slice and Hook Trap

You can have the best swing in the world, but if your clubface isn't square at the moment of truth, the ball is going to curve. Most golfers fight a slice or a hook for years because they can't actually see where their driver is pointing when they stand over the ball.

  • 1
    Drivers Are Hard to Aim

    Drivers have no flat edges or aiming guides making aiming the clubface a complete guessing game. Imagine trying to level a picture frame that is an oval instead of a square.

  • 2
    Small Miss, Big Trouble

    A miss that looks small at your feet becomes massive 250 yards away. What feels like a slight tweak often ends up 40 yards deep in the rough.

Open or closed by just 2 degrees — your ball ends up in completely different zip codes.

The Solution

Face Pointers

Face pointer have been around forever and simply use magnets to attach to the club face. You then use the pointer as a visual guide to make sure your face is square to the target line at impact.

  • 1
    Magnets Don't Work

    Today's high-performance drivers are made of titanium and carbon fiber which are not magnetic. So the traditional magnetic face pointers fail. to stick!

  • 2
    A Tool Is Just a Tool Without Instruction

    Online resources are scatter and focus on the swing instead of the club face. Drive Align inlcudes a simple, step-by-step guide that teaches you how to use the tool to fix your aim and start hitting straighter drives.

A square face at impact is the difference between a fairway and a phone call to find your ball.
